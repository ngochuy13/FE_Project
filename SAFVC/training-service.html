Training & Call Up

There are three key considerations that guide the design of training for the SAFVC. Firstly, training that the volunteers undertake will be safe. Secondly, training will be realistic and engaging. Finally the SAFVC will endeavour to be good stewards of the volunteers’ time. Training pedagogies will enhance training realism and effectiveness and enable SAFVC to achieve the instructional intent.

Basic Training

Basic Training (Continuous Option - over two weeks)

Basic Training is a common module for all SAFVC Volunteer trainees and it aims to provide them a basic understanding and appreciation of the military ethos and values of the SAF. They will be equipped with basic soldiering skills and knowledge. The volunteers will be trained to fire the Singapore Assault Rifle 21 (SAR21) and learn individual fieldcraft to adapt to field environment. Basic Training will culminate in a field camp.

Basic Training (Continuous Option) is a two-week stay-in programme.

Basic Training (Modular Option – over ten Saturdays)

Volunteers who are unable to join Basic Training (Continuous Option) may choose to participate in Basic Training (Modular), conducted over ten Saturdays. The training syllabus will be comparable to Basic Training (Continuous).

Qualification Training

Qualification Training (Continuous Option - over one week)

Upon graduating from Basic Training, all volunteers will undergo Qualification Training. It aims to provide them with the necessary skills and knowledge to perform in their role. Qualification Training is conducted by the respective SAF formations. For example, those who choose to contribute as a military nurse will undergo Qualification Training at the SAF Medical Training Institute. Volunteers will be promoted from SV(Trainee) to SV1 upon the conclusion of Qualification Training, signifying that they are ready to serve in a uniformed capacity.

Qualification Training (Continuous Option) is a stay-in programme that will take up to five days.

Qualification Training (Modular Option – over five Saturdays)

Volunteers who are unable to join Qualification Training (Continuous Option) may choose to participate in Qualification Training (Modular), which will be conducted over five Saturdays. The training syllabus will be comparable to Qualification Training (Continuous).

Advanced Training

Advanced Training (Continuous Option - over one week)

Some roles* require more training and SAFVC Volunteers will undergo Advanced Training to gain the required proficiencies. For example, those who are deployed as Auxiliary Security Troopers will need additional training in weapon handling and marksmanship. They are also required to undertake specialised training such as Rules of Engagement and Military Police Close Combat Training in order to serve alongside our SAF soldiers. Find out more in the Roles section.

Advanced Training (Continuous Option) is a stay-in programme that will take up to five days.

Advanced Training (Modular Option – over five Saturdays)

SAFVC Volunteers who are unable to join Advanced Training (Continuous Option) may choose to participate in Advanced Training (Modular Option), which will be conducted over five Saturdays. The training syllabus will be comparable to Advanced Training (Continuous Option).

*The roles that require Advanced Training are: Auxiliary Security Trooper, Bridge Watchkeeper and Deck Operator (Seamanship).

Call-Up

Volunteers can only be deployed in a role once they have completed Qualification Training (and where applicable, Advanced Training). They can apply to complete Basic Training first, and take on Qualification Training at a later date.

SAFVC Volunteers will be called up for training & deployment through the SAF100V to typically serve seven days in a year. The SAF100V grants them statutory protection from dismissal arising from their call-ups. It will also be the responsibility of the volunteers to inform their employers of scheduled SAFVC activities early to allow for arrangements to be made for someone to cover their duties during the call-up.
