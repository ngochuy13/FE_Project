National Service: The Cornerstone of Singapore’s Defence and Security

We live in a prosperous nation with a thriving economy, world-class amenities and a highly educated population. That we can go about pursuing our dreams is underwritten by the men and women of the Singapore Armed Forces whose mission is to enhance Singapore’s peace and security through deterrence and diplomacy – and should these fail, to secure a swift and decisive victory over the aggressor.

The men and women of the SAF: NSFs (Full-time National Servicemen), NSmen and regulars work tirelessly to ensure the peace and security of Singapore. It is a noble and onerous duty. Perhaps you, too, wish to join us in ensuring the peace and security of our home. Whether you have specific skills that are directly applicable in the SAF, or you are motivated by the desire to make a contribution to Singapore’s defence and security, the SAF Volunteer Corps welcomes you to join us.

Why Join The SAF Volunteer Corps (SAFVC)

The SAF Volunteer Corps is a uniformed volunteer scheme introduced to provide more Singaporeans and Permanent Residents with the opportunity to contribute to national defence, show support for National Service and deepen their understanding and ownership of national defence. You will undergo progressive training to ensure you are competent and ready to serve alongside our NSF, NSmen and regulars in roles that support SAF’s operations and training.

Be prepared to learn to work as a team, become stronger and more resilient as you begin your journey in the SAFVC. As you serve as a SAFVC Volunteer, don't be surprised when family and friends observe a more mature and confident you. The invaluable experience and skillsets that you gain in the SAFVC will put you in good stead as you progress through life.
Join the SAFVC and play your part in national defence.

SAFVC Volunteer Mission and Vision

Steadfast and Vigilant
Our Mission

The mission of SAFVC Volunteers is to serve in assigned roles alongside SAF NSFs, NSmen and regulars to enhance the security of Singapore.

Our Vision

To be reliable, respected and resilient volunteers who are competent, confident and committed, ready to serve to enhance the peace and security of Singapore.

SAFVC Logo and Motto

SAFVC Logo

The SAFVC logo is inspired by the rich heritage of the Singapore Volunteer Corps (SVC). It retains the circular shield, banner and laurel of the original SVC logo which together with the lion, portray power and courage. The lion's statant posture with its head facing forward and tail curved over its back, symbolises the steadfastness and vigilance of the SAFVC Volunteers.

The stylised outline of Singapore above the lion is a fitting reminder that our country is now a proud and independent state that relies on her people for defence. The gold of the logo, being the colour of the brass in our ammunition, signifies the martial nature of the SAFVC. This, together with the deep blue background, symbolises the common foundation of the SAFVC and the SAF.

SAFVC Motto - Steadfast & Vigilant

The SAFVC is steadfast; drawing on the spirit, fortitude and resilience of the SAFVC Volunteers to stand united with our NSFs and NSmen in defending Singapore.

The SAFVC is vigilant; a credible and committed force that is ever watchful and never tiring in our mission to play our part to help protect Singapore.

Home of the SAFVC

The SAFVC will be based in Maju Camp, which is located off Clementi Road. Maju Camp will house the SAFVC Headquarters, which will function as the Formation HQ of the SAFVC. Basic Training will also be conducted in Maju Camp for all SAFVC Volunteers.

Overview of Service

SVs (SAFVC Volunteers) will be put through four progressive training phases in the SAFVC. Each phase is designed to develop our SVs and maintain their competencies to allow them to serve alongside our NSF, NSmen and regulars in a supporting capacity.

  • Experience Phase (3 Years)

    SVs will complete their Basic, Qualification, and where applicable, Advanced Training. After completion of training, SVs will be adequately prepared for their various roles and will be deployed to perform basic tasks under the close supervision of experienced commanders. This will build basic job proficiencies.

  • Perform Phase (3 Years)

    SVs may be deployed to perform more challenging roles, with less supervision. Currency and skills training will keep SVs engaged, as well as improve their skills.

  • Lead Phase (4 Years)

    Leadership modules will be introduced to equip selected SVs with the skills to take on leadership roles within SAFVC.

  • Mentor Phase

    Senior SVs, with more than 10 years of experience, may take up mentor or trainer roles within the SAFVC.
